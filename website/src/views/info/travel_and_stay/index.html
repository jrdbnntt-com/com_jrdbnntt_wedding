Travel & Stay

Things to Do

We are excited that some of our out-of-state friends and family will be enjoying the Pacific Northwest for the first time. Here are some of our favorite restaurants, outings, and activities.

Brunch

  • Closest to the wedding venue: Woodblock in Redmond
  • Best vegetarian: Cafe Flora in Madison Park, Seattle
  • Best in the Seattle area: Dreamland in Fremont, Seattle

Restaurants

  • Hawaiian food: Kona Kitchen in Green Lake
  • Mexican & Bar: Matador in Ballard
  • Fast burgers: Dick’s Drive In (all around Seattle)

Seattle Things to Do

  • Museum of Pop Culture (MoPOP) - a museum dedicated to not just pop culture, but also Seattle music legends such as Pearl Jam, Jimi Hendrix, and Nirvana. They also have excellent exhibits dedicated to Sci-FI, fantasy, and Hip-Hop music.
  • Cafe Mox (Ballard or Bellevue) - a restaurant/board game store, Mox allows you to play board games while eating delicious food, with great coffee and cocktails depending on the mood.
  • Flatstick Pub - indoor mini-golf with alcohol! What else can you ask for?

Parks

  • Green Lake - a massive lake in the middle of the city, this is a great spot for people watching.
  • Washington Park Arboretum / UW Botanical Garden - this is where Hannah and Jared had their engagement photos taken, and is absolutely a must-see for garden and flower lovers!
  • University of Washington campus - not a park, but a beautiful outdoor space nonetheless! Walk around the quad where Hannah spent many hours procrastinating on homework!
  • Gasworks Park - the BEST view of the city, and a great picnic spot.

Day Trips

  • Whidbey Island - a ferry ride away, Whidbey is Hannah and Jared’s go-to getaway and is a beautiful retreat from the city. It’s the perfect destination for an easy day of driving to see the sights and visit the cute towns. Notable food spots include Gordon’s on Blueberry Hill for high-end dining and Braeburn for an excellent brunch experience.
  • Orcas Island - another ferry ride away, it is the perfect destination for camping, hiking, and other outdoor activities. It also has a cute town called Eastsound for all your needs while you camp.
  • Snoqualmie Falls - a PNW classic, this and the greater Snoqualmie Pass is less than an hour from Seattle. For fans of waterfalls and Twin Peaks alike!
  • La Conner - this was the destination of Hannah & Jared’s first day trip together. Less than 2 hours a way, it is a cute town with shops, food, and a walkable waterfront. Be sure to cross the Rainbow Bridge!

COVID-19

We hope to have a night that everyone remembers, but not as a superspreader event. As hosts, we are taking all steps to ensure it is as safe as possible, including making it an entirely outdoor event.

We are requiring all guests, including plus ones, to be fully vaccinated and to show proof of vaccination upon arrival.

If resources permit, we will rapid-test all guests. If you are unable or unwilling to be tested or show proof of vaccination, we respectfully ask for you to not attend.

We are currently planning for a full event, but we reserve the right to downsize, reschedule, or cancel in response to the pandemic. Please follow your comfort level and perceptions of risk when choosing to attend. Masks will not be required, but encouraged as a personal choice.
