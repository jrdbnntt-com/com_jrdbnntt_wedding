Seattle Things to Do

Museum of Pop Culture (MoPOP) - a museum dedicated to not just pop culture, but also Seattle music legends such as Pearl Jam, Jimi Hendrix, and Nirvana. They also have excellent exhibits dedicated to Sci-FI, fantasy, and Hip-Hop music.

Cafe Mox (Ballard or Bellevue) - a restaurant/board game store, Mox allows you to play board games while eating delicious food, with great coffee and cocktails depending on the mood.

Flatstick Pub - indoor mini-golf with alcohol! What else can you ask for?

CSZ Seattle - drink and watch improv! This is where Jared took all of his improv classes and might preform occasionally in the future.

Local Parks


Washington Park Arboretum / UW Botanical Garden - this is where Hannah and Jared had their engagement photos taken, and is absolutely a must-see for garden and flower lovers!

Green Lake - a massive lake in the middle of the city, this is a great spot for people watching.

University of Washington campus - not a park, but a beautiful outdoor space nonetheless! Walk around the quad where Hannah spent many hours procrastinating on homework!

Gasworks Park - the BEST view of the city, and a great picnic spot.

Day Trips


Literally any mountain or island trail - they are everywhere! We've got the olympics to the west and the cascades to the east. The islands even have mountains. They are always fun, and you can't beat the views!

Whidbey Island - a ferry ride away, Whidbey is Hannah and Jared’s go-to getaway and is a beautiful retreat from the city. It’s the perfect destination for an easy day of driving to see the sights and visit the cute towns. Notable food spots include Gordon’s on Blueberry Hill for high-end dining and Braeburn for an excellent brunch experience. If you are up for a nice long drive, ferry to the southern tip of the island and then drive up along the coast to Deception Pass.

Orcas Island - another ferry ride away, it is the perfect destination for camping, hiking, and other outdoor activities. It also has a cute town called Eastsound for all your needs while you camp.

La Conner - this was the destination of Hannah & Jared’s first day trip together. Less than 2 hours a way, it is a cute town with shops, food, and a walkable waterfront. Be sure to cross the Rainbow Bridge!

Snoqualmie Falls - a PNW classic, this and the greater Snoqualmie Pass is less than an hour from Seattle. For fans of waterfalls and Twin Peaks alike!

Mt. Rainier - the big mountain visible from Seattle! There are a lot of easy hiking trails and gorgeous sites.

Olympic National Park - on the western peninsula of the state, this is one of Jared's favorite national parks in the country. Great for hiking, driving, and camping! You'll likely want to spend more than just a day here if you go, but it will be worth it!
